## Overview

The Supplier Management module allows you to maintain a comprehensive database of your suppliers, including their contact information, payment terms, and transaction history. This is essential for efficient purchasing and inventory management.

<Info>
Supplier data is used throughout the system when creating purchase orders and tracking inventory sources.
</Info>

## Adding a New Supplier

To register a new supplier in the system:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Supplier Dialog">
    Click the **New Supplier** button to open the supplier creation form.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enter Business Information">
    Fill in the required business details:
    - **Business Name**: Legal name of the supplier company
    - **Tax ID**: Tax identification number (required)
    - **Contact Name**: Primary contact person at the supplier
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add Contact Details">
    Provide communication information:
    - **Phone**: Office phone number
    - **Mobile**: Mobile contact number (required)
    - **Email**: Primary email address (required)
    - **City**: Supplier's city location
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Set Payment Terms">
    Configure the payment terms:
    - **Payment Term**: Number of days for payment (e.g., 30, 60, 90 days)
    - **Status**: Toggle to activate/deactivate the supplier
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save Supplier">
    Click **Save** to register the supplier in your database.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
All fields except phone are required. The system validates that all mandatory information is provided before saving.
</Note>

## Editing Supplier Information

To update an existing supplier's information:

1. Locate the supplier in the suppliers table
2. Click the **Edit** button next to the supplier
3. Modify the desired fields in the form
4. Click **Save** to update the information

<Tip>
The system clones the supplier data when editing to prevent live updates to the table while you're making changes.
</Tip>

## Searching and Filtering

The supplier list includes a powerful search feature that filters across multiple fields:

```typescript
// Search filters across:
- Business Name
- Tax ID
- Contact Name
- City
- Email
```

Simply type in the search box, and the list will automatically filter to show matching suppliers.

## Supplier Status Management

Each supplier can be marked as active or inactive:

- **Active** (true): Supplier appears in purchase order dropdowns
- **Inactive** (false): Supplier is hidden from active selections but retained for historical records

<Warning>
Deactivating a supplier doesn't delete historical purchase records associated with them.
</Warning>

## Permission Requirements

The Suppliers module uses role-based permissions:

| Permission | Description |
|------------|-------------|
| `canView` | View the suppliers list |
| `canCreate` | Add new suppliers |
| `canEdit` | Modify existing suppliers |
| `canDelete` | Remove suppliers from the system |
| `canExport` | Export supplier data |

<Info>
Permissions are configured per user role in the **Proveedores** (Suppliers) module.
</Info>

## Integration with Purchases

Suppliers are automatically available in the purchase order system:

- Select a supplier when creating a purchase order
- View all purchases made from a specific supplier
- Track payment terms and due dates based on supplier settings

## Best Practices

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Maintain Accurate Contact Information">
    Keep supplier contact details up to date to ensure smooth communication for orders, deliveries, and payments.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Set Realistic Payment Terms">
    Configure payment terms that match your actual agreements with suppliers. This helps with cash flow planning and payment tracking.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Use Descriptive Business Names">
    Use clear, searchable business names to make it easy to find suppliers when creating purchase orders.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Regular Data Cleanup">
    Periodically review and deactivate suppliers you no longer work with, rather than deleting them to preserve historical data.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
