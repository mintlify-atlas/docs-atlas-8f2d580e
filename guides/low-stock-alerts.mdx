## Overview

The Low Stock Alerts system helps you maintain optimal inventory levels by monitoring stock against configurable thresholds. The system provides real-time visibility into products that need reordering, preventing stockouts and lost sales.

<Info>
Low stock alerts are calculated automatically based on your minimum stock settings for each product and warehouse.
</Info>

## Alert Levels

The system categorizes products into three alert levels:

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Critical" icon="circle-exclamation" color="#ef4444">
    Stock at or below critical threshold (default: 5 units)
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Low" icon="exclamation-triangle" color="#f97316">
    Stock at or below minimum stock level
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Warning" icon="exclamation" color="#eab308">
    Stock approaching minimum level
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Dashboard Overview

The low stock dashboard provides quick visibility:

### Summary Cards

At the top of the screen, you'll see real-time counts:

- **Critical Items**: Products requiring immediate attention
- **Low Items**: Products below minimum stock
- **Warning Items**: Products approaching reorder point
- **Unread Alerts**: New critical alerts

### Alert Status Calculation

```typescript
// Status determination logic:
if (available <= criticalThreshold) {
  status = 'critical';
} else if (available <= minStock) {
  status = 'low';
} else {
  status = 'warning';
}
```

## Alert Settings Configuration

Customize how the alert system behaves:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Settings">
    Click the **Settings** or gear icon to open the alert configuration dialog.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Configure Thresholds">
    Set your alert thresholds:
    - **Critical Threshold**: Stock level triggering critical alerts (default: 5)
    - **Low Threshold**: Stock level for low alerts (default: 10)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enable Notifications">
    Toggle notification options:
    - **Email Notifications**: Receive email alerts for low stock
    - **Auto Reorder**: Automatically create purchase suggestions (future)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save Settings">
    Click **Save** to apply your configuration.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
Changing thresholds will immediately recategorize all products based on the new values.
</Warning>

## Setting Minimum Stock Levels

Minimum stock levels are configured per product and warehouse:

1. Go to **Inventory Management**
2. Edit a product
3. In the warehouse section, set:
   - **Min Stock**: Minimum quantity before alert
   - **Max Stock**: Maximum quantity for optimal stock

<Tip>
Set minimum stock based on:
- Lead time from supplier
- Average daily sales
- Safety stock buffer
- Product seasonality
</Tip>

## Reordering Process

When you identify products needing reorder:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Review Low Stock List">
    Identify products requiring immediate reordering based on status.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Create Purchase Order">
    Navigate to **Purchases** and create a new purchase order.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add Products">
    Add the low stock products to your purchase order.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Determine Quantities">
    Calculate order quantity:
    ```
    Order Qty = Max Stock - Current Stock
    ```
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Submit to Supplier">
    Complete and save the purchase order.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Stock Level Management

Best practices for maintaining healthy stock levels:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Regular Monitoring">
    Check the low stock dashboard daily or weekly depending on your business volume. Critical items should be addressed immediately.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="ABC Analysis">
    Categorize products by importance:
    - **A items**: High-value, set tighter controls
    - **B items**: Medium-value, standard monitoring
    - **C items**: Low-value, can have higher variance
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Seasonal Adjustments">
    Adjust minimum stock levels seasonally for products with variable demand patterns.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Supplier Lead Time">
    Set minimum stock to cover the full lead time plus a safety buffer:
    ```
    Min Stock = (Daily Sales × Lead Time Days) + Safety Stock
    ```
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Regular Reviews">
    Review and update minimum/maximum stock levels quarterly based on actual sales patterns.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Preventing Stockouts

Proactive stock management strategies:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Set Realistic Minimums">
    Configure minimum stock levels that account for supplier lead times and sales velocity.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Monitor Critical Items Daily">
    Check critical alerts every day and initiate orders immediately.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Establish Reorder Routines">
    Create a regular schedule for reviewing and placing orders (e.g., every Monday).
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Use Safety Stock">
    Build in safety stock buffers for your best-selling or most critical products.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Track Lead Times">
    Monitor actual supplier lead times and adjust minimum stock accordingly.
  </Step>
</Steps>
